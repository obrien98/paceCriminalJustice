Our Purpose

The Criminal Justice Society gives students a chance to go beyond lectures and actually engage with the field. Through events, discussions, and guest speakers, members get a clearer picture of what careers in criminal justice look like day to day.

Whether you're planning a career in law enforcement, the legal system, or public service, CJS helps you explore your options while meeting other students with similar interests.

Why Students Join CJS

CJS is a place to get involved, meet people, and start figuring out where you fit in the field.

Career Exploration

Hear from professionals and alumni so you can compare pathways in law enforcement, legal studies, corrections, investigations, and community-based justice work.

Networking That Feels Real

Meet faculty, guest speakers, and other students who can help shape your academic decisions, internship plans, and long-term goals.

Hands-On Learning

Take part in panels, workshops, site visits, and discussions that bring criminal justice topics out of the textbook and into real-world context.

Leadership Development

Develop communication, planning, and teamwork skills by helping shape programming and leading initiatives that matter to the student body.

Community and Belonging

Join a community of students who care about justice, public service, and thoughtful conversations about the systems that shape our communities.

Resume and Internship Readiness

Build stronger experience through involvement, event participation, leadership, and the professional confidence that comes from showing up consistently.

Where CJS Can Take You

Students join with different goals. The club is designed to help you explore multiple directions while building a stronger understanding of the field as a whole.

Law Enforcement

Learn about agency culture, recruitment, and public-facing service roles.

Courts and Legal Systems

Explore how courtroom processes, legal institutions, and justice policy intersect.

Corrections and Rehabilitation

Understand reentry, supervision, rehabilitation, and the broader corrections system.

Advocacy and Public Service

See how justice work connects to community support, reform, and impact beyond enforcement.
